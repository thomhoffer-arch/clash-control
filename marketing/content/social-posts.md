### Post 2 — Feature Spotlight: Walk Mode + Sheets (Week 2)
**Format:** Text + demo screenshot

---

Two questions I get a lot about BIM coordination:

"Can I walk through the model to review clash context?" → Yes.
"Can I export annotated floor plans for the site team?" → Yes.

ClashControl v4.14 added both.

**Walk mode** lets you move through the model at eye height — useful for visualizing clearance clashes in mechanical rooms and corridors. Unit-aware speed, configurable eye height.

**Sheets** generate annotated floor plan cuts with labeled elements, configurable cut height and view depth, and DXF export for CAD teams who don't use a 3D viewer.

Free. Open source. Runs in a browser tab.

**Format:** Personal long-form post

The most common reaction when someone first tries ClashControl is: "Wait, this is actually free?"

Yes. All of it. The clash detection, the BCF export, the AI triage, the natural language commands, the walk mode, the annotated sheets. Free. No freemium tier. No "upgrade to unlock."

Here's why.

BIM coordination is already hard. Coordinating between disciplines, managing issue backlogs, running federation meetings, chasing consultants for updated models — there's plenty of friction built into the process.

### r/BIM — Show & Tell Post (Week 3)

**Title:** I built a free, browser-based IFC clash detection tool — here's what it can do [OC]

**Body:**
Hey r/BIM — I've been working on ClashControl for a while and thought this community might find it useful.

It's a free, open-source clash detection app that runs entirely in your browser. No install, no Autodesk account, no license fee.

**What it does:**
- Load multiple IFC 2x3/4 models (drag & drop)
- Hard clash and soft clash detection with configurable clearance
- 3D viewer with orbit, section planes, measurement tools
- AI-generated clash titles and severity classification
- Natural language commands ("show critical clashes on Level 3")
- BCF 2.1 export (opens in Revit, BIMcollab, etc.)
- Walk mode and annotated floor plan cuts with DXF export
- Works offline as a PWA

The whole app is a single HTML file. Open it locally or go to clashcontrol.io. Source is on GitHub (MIT license).

Happy to answer questions about how it works or what's on the roadmap. Screenshots in comments.
