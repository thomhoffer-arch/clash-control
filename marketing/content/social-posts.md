### Post 2 — Walk Mode + Sheets spotlight (Week 2)
**Format:** Text + demo screenshot

---

Two things that used to require separate tools. Now both free.

**Walk mode** — step into your IFC model at eye height and move through it. Not orbit. Not a fly-through animation. First-person navigation, so you can actually stand in a mechanical room and look at whether a clearance clash matters in context.

**Sheets** — cut your model at any height, annotate it, and export as DXF. For every site team, contractor, and coordinator who has AutoCAD open and isn't going to load a 3D model just to find where the duct is.

Both landed in ClashControl v4.14, alongside IDS import/export, discipline-colored outlines, and smarter soft clash markers that actually sit at the closest point between elements.

### r/BIM — Show & Tell (Week 3)

**Title:** Built a free, open-source IFC clash detection tool that runs in a browser — here's what v4.14 can do [OC]

**Body:**

Hey r/BIM — I've been building ClashControl for a while and this community has been on my mind as I add features, so here's a proper show-and-tell.

**What it is:** Free, open-source clash detection. Runs entirely in your browser. Single HTML file — no install, no vendor account, no license.

**What it does:**

- Load multiple IFC 2x3/4 models (drag and drop)
- Hard clash and soft clash detection, configurable clearance
- AI-generated clash titles in plain English — not "CLASH_0047"
- Natural language commands: type "show me critical clashes on Level 3" and it just works
- BCF 2.1 export — opens in Revit, BIMcollab, Solibri, Navisworks
- Walk mode: first-person navigation at eye height for clash context
- Sheets: annotated floor plan cuts with DXF export
- IDS import/export for data quality validation
- Discipline-colored outlines (MEP = red, structural = blue, arch = purple)
- Offline PWA, works without internet after first load

**What it doesn't do (yet):** It won't replace a full issue management platform for a 50-person project team, and for exact mesh-to-mesh intersection on very complex geometry there's an optional local Python engine addon. OBB-based detection handles the vast majority of coordination workflows.

Source: github.com/clashcontrol-io/clashcontrol (MIT license)
App: clashcontrol.io

Screenshots in comments. Happy to answer any questions about the architecture or roadmap.
